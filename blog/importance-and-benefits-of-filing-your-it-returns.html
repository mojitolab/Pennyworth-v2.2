Importance and Benefits of filing your IT returns

  • April 16, 2020

Many Indian citizens assume filing Income Tax Returns is voluntary, and therefore think it as burdensome and unnecessary. Filing ITR is beneficial in many ways and keeps you tax compliant.

TLet us know the importance of ITR filing along with the benefits. (I’ll not bore with sections/numbers, so read on!)



  • ITR receipt ensures a hassle-free loan processing: Many financial institutes ask for ITR receipts of the latest three(3) years as a supporting document while you apply for a loan. Because ITR has detailed information about your income and acts as a reliable piece of document for the banks to confirm your eligibility for a loan. If you’re planning for that home/car loans, start filing your ITR!
  • Filing returns is mandatory in some cases: Even though you are exempted from filing ITR, it is a good idea to so. In India, if you want to claim the paid charges for registration and stamp duty is possible if you file returns in that financial year. Both an individual and a HUF(Hindu Undivided Family) can claim this deduction in their income tax return.
  • Hassle-free Visa Processing: If you have plans of studying or working abroad, it is best you start filing IT returns. Most of the Embassies ask ITR filings as a document to confirm that you’ll be able to afford to live in their country. This just makes the whole process of acquiring a visa seamless.
  • It is a sign you are responsible: Although a voluntary action, filing ITR proves that you are a responsible citizen. It is beneficial to you in multiple ways, unlike the popular misconceptions. So be a responsible citizen, file your IT returns and become tax compliant.


To add on:

  • ITR filing avoids Penalties
  • ITR Receipt is a very important document in many cases
  • Claiming adjustment against past losses
  • Useful when you need revised returns


Also, people are under the misconception that exempted candidates don’t miss out on anything when they don’t file ITR. But, there are a lot of benefits one misses out as pointed out in this article.
