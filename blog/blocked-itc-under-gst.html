Blocked ITC under GST

  • June 19, 2020

The Goods and Service Tax (GST) is known for coming into picture for easy movement of Input Tax Credit, thereby removing cascading effects of taxes. However, section 17(5) of the CGST Act, 2017 specifies certain Goods and Services as Blocked Credit. It means, an assessee will not be eligible to take Input Tax Credit on the goods and services specified under this section of the Act.

neligible Input Tax Credits or Blocked Credits is a cost to the company. The business owners will further pass on this cost to the customers/end users, to maintain their profit percentage. This will lead to increased costs of products/services for which ITC is blocked which will cause inflation in some fields of the economy.

Cases which are not eligible for ITC under GST:

  • Motor Vehicles: Motor Vehicles for transportation of persons having approved seating capacity of not more than 13 persons (including driver). This includes leasing, renting or hiring of the aforesaid vehicle.
    ITC is not available on vessels and aircrafts too.
    ITC would be blocked in respect of services of general insurance, servicing, repairs and maintenance of the above mentioned motor vehicles, vessels or aircrafts. Hence, if a car is bought for business purposes, ITC cannot be claimed on it.
    ITC would be available if motor vehicles/vessels/aircrafts is used for making the following taxable supplies:

    • 1. If you are in the business of supplying vehicles.
      Example – If Mr. A, a car dealer purchases the car for Rs. 30 lakhs plus 8.4 lakhs GST, and later the same car was further sold for Rs. 40 lakhs along with Rs. 11.2 lakhs GST, he can claim ITC of Rs. 8.4 lakhs, since he is a dealer, and pay only Rs. 2.8 lakhs GST (11.2 – 8.4)
    • 2. If you are in the business of providing transportation to passengers, then the vehicle purchased for the aforesaid use would be eligible for ITC.
    • 3. Imparting training on driving, flying, navigating such vehicle or vessel or aircraft. For example, if a driving school purchases a car to train its students, then ITC can be claimed on the car purchased.
    • 4. If a vehicle is purchased to transport goods from one place to another, ITC can be claimed on the same. However, this does not apply to Goods Transport Agencies.

    ITC would further be allowed for leasing, renting or hiring of vehicles, when they are used for the above said purposes (a to d) or when the recipient is involved in the manufacture of such vehicles or in the supply of general insurance services with regard to such vehicles insured by him.

  • Food, beverages, club memberships and others: ITC cannot be claimed for the following goods or services or both:

    • 1. Food and beverages
    • 2. Outdoor catering
    • 3. Beauty treatment
    • 4. Health services
    • 5. Cosmetic and plastic surgery

    However, ITC can be claimed if a registered person uses inward supply of the aforesaid goods or services or both of a particular category for making an outward taxable supply of the same category of goods or services or both or as an element of a taxable composite or mixed supply.


    Examples:

    • 1. ABC Company hires an outdoor caterer for celebrating its annual day. ABC Company here is not eligible for ITC.
    • 2. When an airline company purchases packed food for the passengers, then ITC can be claimed for inward supply. It is considered as a part of the composite supply of air travel service.
    • 3. If any production house is taking beauty treatment then ITC can be claimed on this service.
    • 4. A restaurant taking inward supply of beverages can claim ITC.

  • Sale of membership in a club, health and fitness centre: ITC cannot be claimed for any membership fees paid for gyms, clubs, etc.
    XYZ Company takes gym membership for its employees. ITC cannot be claimed.

  • Rent-a-cab, health insurance, life insurance: No ITC would be available on the aforesaid services.

    Following are the exceptions to this rule:


    • 1. Any above service which is made compulsory for the employer to provide its employee by the Government under any current law in force.
    • 2. If the category is the same for the inward and outward supply or it is part of mixed or composite supply.
    • 3. ITC would be admissible for motor vehicles purchased for demo for sales promotion as they are for the purpose of business.

    Examples:

    • 1. If the government made a rule that health insurance is mandatory for all employees, then the company taking out health insurance of all its employees would be eligible for ITC.
    • 2.XYZ Travels rent-a-cab service to AB travels. Then AB Travels can claim ITC.

  • Travel Benefits: ITC cannot be claimed on travel benefits given to employees on vacation such as leaves or any home travel concessions.
    However, ITC will be allowed if the travel is for business purposes.

  • Works Contract: ITC would not be allowed for construction of immovable property, except when the input service is used for further work contract services.
    Here “construction” includes reconstruction, renovation, additions or alterations or repairs to the extent of capitalisation to the said immovable property.
    Which means, ITC can be availed if expenses of renovation, repairs, reconstruction or alterations are not capitalized in the books of account
    ITC cannot be availed for goods or services for construction of an immovable property on his own account, even if such goods or services are used in the course of further business.
    One exception to this is plants and machinery. It means ITC can be claimed in inputs used to manufacture plants and machinery for own use.

    Examples:

    • 1. If a company hires a contractor to construct an office premises, he is not eligible for ITC.
    • 2. If the contractor has subcontracted the work to another contractor, then the contractor is eligible for ITC for the sub-contact service.
    • 3. Manufacturing Company taking service for construction of AC plant in the factory, would be eligible for ITC since it is considered as plant & machinery.
    • 4. ABC Ltd is the developer of buildings. It builds an office for itself. ITC cannot be claimed even if the office is used for business purposes.
    • 5. ABC Ltd constructs boilers for itself. ITC can be claimed as it is plant & machinery.

  • Composition Scheme: ITC cannot be claimed on goods or services or both on which tax has been paid under the composition scheme of GST Act.

  • Non-Residents: Goods or services or both received by a non-resident taxable person are not eligible for ITC, except for goods imported by him.

  • Personal Use: ITC would be blocked for the goods or services used for personal purpose and not for business purpose.

  • Destroyed goods and Free samples: Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off or given off as gift or free samples will not be eligible for ITC.
    Such goods are not being used for providing taxable supplies, hence ITC is blocked.

    Examples:

    • 1. ABC Ltd purchased 50 rolls of yarns to manufacture cloth. But due to the accident to the truck 10 yarns are destroyed and 10 yarns are stolen. Hence, 20 yarns would not be eligible for ITC.
    • 2. ITC would not be available when gold coins are distributed among customers under any scheme.
    • 3. ITC cannot be claimed on GST paid on expenses incurred towards promotional schemes like foreign trip or gifts for achieving targeted sales.

  • Fraud Cases: No ITC would be eligible for any tax paid due to fraud cases which has resulted into:

    • 1. Non or short payment of tax.
    • 2. Excessive refund.
    • 3. ITC utilised.

    Fraud cases will include fraud or wilful misstatements or suppression of facts or confiscation and seizure of goods.
    In such cases, tax was not paid with an intention to evade tax, hence in order to penalize such assessments ITC has been blocked.

  • Restaurants: Restaurants charge GST to the customers but cannot claim ITC on it.
    This rule is applicable to only stand-alone restaurants and not to restaurants as a part of hotels with room tariffs exceeding Rs. 7500.

    Example:
    • 1. Burger King charges 5% GST and cannot claim ITC.
    • 2. Taj’s Grill by the Pool restaurant in Kolkata is a part of the Taj Bengal hotel and so it will charge 18% GST while enjoying ITC.


It is imperative to take care of the above cases where Input Tax Credit is not available, as if ITC claimed in the cases where it is not eligible, it will be considered as Excess ITC Claimed and the same would be added to output tax liability in the succeeding month with Interest. Period of interest would be from the date of availing ITC till the date of addition to output tax liability.
