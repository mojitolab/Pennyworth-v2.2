Compliance Audit – A growing requirement

  • July 20, 2020

Compliance Audit is a type of audit service. It focuses mainly on the organization’s or entity’s compliance with the local laws, the regulations and the related rules. It also keeps in check the company’s or entity’s internal rules, their regulations, terms and conditions, their procedures. It makes sure not adhering to the policies and decisions is met with penalties or fines which can be monetary amounts or close operations.

Generally, the compliance audit is performed against requirements as follows:

  • Local Laws and Regulations: The organization/entity needs to make sure that all the operations are in agreement with the laws. They are obliged to set up business processes and valid procedures. Their decisions should include advice from a legal consultant.
    They must have a legal department to check on their noteworthy processes. It can help in making sure penalty if any is minimized and all the procedures are in compliance with the local laws and rules.
    They might need an internal audit department to check on the law requirements of their specific locality. This committee is responsible for the documentation of the entity/organization.
  • Business Regulations: Apart from local laws and regulations, compliance auditing also includes reviewing the organization’s frameworks and policies. For example, if an organization expands their business to other countries, the requirements of other countries should be met and obeyed.
    The compliance auditing also includes review of current practices of the organization and if found sparse, interdepartmental meetings should take place where the methods and procedures are corrected and re-established accordingly.
  • Organization’s policies, processes and procedures: A compliance audit is also performed on the organization's policies, processes and procedures. They are deemed very important for the growth of the organization and hence hold high value. Failing to which there can be a wastage on the usage of time and resources which sometimes can also be considered as a deception. Strict actions are taken against this. Usually, a special subset team of compliance auditing committee separately works on this.


Who performs the compliance Audit?

The compliance audit is performed sometimes by the internal auditing committee and also by external auditing committees. Internal committees work under the supervision of the company and are concave. When there are problems like whistle blowers, leakage of information or services within internal audit committees there is a call for external audit committees.

The final compliance auditing report is passed on to the associated department, or the CEO and the CFO of the respective company. That is further directed to the board of directors and the auditing committee. In large organizations the resulting changes by the audit are enforced in every department, division and unit and strict policies, procedures and rules are imposed accordingly.
