Valuation

Valuation is of significant importance to companies especially in the wake of the changing global regulatory requirements and evolving market dynamics. Valuation is critical for financial reporting under Ind AS, benchmarking studies, statutory reporting of inbound and outbound investments, issuance of ESOPs etc. Often negotiations or internal management decisions, transactions, mergers, acquisitions, etc also necessitate valuation.

Valuation facilitates decision making by enabling us to determine whether the particular decision will be able to meet with our expectation. Valuation is needed in:

Portfolio Management

Valuation is of significant importance for an active investor. Valuation plays a pivotal role in portfolio management for fundamental analysts, and a peripheral role for technical analysts.

Valuation in Acquisition Analysis

Precise valuation is equally important for both the bidding farm as well as the target firm as both parties aim at maximizing their potential gains. Analysts from both the involved parties need to consider synergy and the value of control to maximize profits for their respective parties.

Valuation in Corporate Finance

Valuation attains larger significance for small and medium enterprises when they seek capital infusion from investors. For large firms, their valuation decides the IPO rate when they decide to go public. While venture capitalists’ expected share in a target firm is estimated based their valuation of the firm, for big business houses valuation determines their decision on investment and borrowing.

Valuation of Company Shares

Valuation of the shares of a Company is needed for multi-fold reasons. Restructuring is the most common scenario here. In restructuring, a member of proprietary company offers a portion of the shares to another member.

Stock Valuation

Stock determines the intrinsic value of a share of a company and generates revenue. Stock valuation predicts the potential market prices for the investors to time their sales or purchase of investments.

It is very important to know the current value of the stock. While for internal matters like MIS, financial statements for internal circulation, it is not compulsory to go for a valuation by professional. However, in case of creating charge on stock, merger transactions, etc. it requires valuation.
