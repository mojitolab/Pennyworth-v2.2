Inbound & Outbound Advisory & FEMA Advisory Services

With the opening up of the world market on the back of global economic reforms, there has been a constant spur in foreign trade and investment between companies across countries. This increase in cross-border transactions involving both inbound and outbound investment makes it imperative for businesses to acquire expertise in business, tax and regulatory aspects of the country where the entity belongs and also of the country of the target company.

Complexities compound with the need to understand and ensure compliance with complex legislation covered under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), which facilitates external trade and payments, and provides guidelines for the dealings of all foreign exchange transactions in India.

FEMA is applicable to:


  • Export & Import of any commodity and/or service
  • Foreign exchange
  • Foreign security
  • Banking, financial and insurance services
  • Purchase, sale and exchange of any kind (i.e. Transfer)
  • Securities as defined under Public Debt Act 1994
  • Any overseas company owned by an NRI and the owner is 60% or more

At Penny Worth Consultants, our team of professionals bring to you extensive first-hand experience from working on multiple national and transborder dealings involving diverse industries in different continents and jurisdictions. We offer profound and dedicated service for any FEMA related issues that encompass the entire gamut of foreign exchange law, ranging from consultancy to statutory compliances.

We provide expert guidance with inbound and outbound investment strategies maximizing structuring for investment into a particular jurisdiction. We hold expertise in strategic, financial and legal guidance in all aspects of inbound acquisition. The scope of services include advices on compliance any other special government regulations specific to the country of the foreign company.

For outbound investment, expert assistance is provided in staying fully aware of any restrictions or obstacles in the conduct of due diligence, specific reporting requirements or other relevant regulation on pre-merger interactions and post-merger implementations.
